Clova

The English name of Clova /kloʊ.və/ comes from the word 'clover', because it is refering to the vegetation of the planet, and frankly, clover is a nice word.

Clova is a planet quite similar to Earth. About 75% of Clova's surface is made up of water. The remaining 25% of it's surface is land, consisting of archipelagos, continents, and islands. A day is 18 hours long. It orbits a star called Hearth.

Clova has a circumference of about 18,500 miles. It's a bit rockier than Earth.

There are many different nations on Clova, for example Engiman, Scant, Zabay, Kalicia, Laychan, Beau, Tayania, Borok, Karethia, Swithin, and less officially, Monuna. (The Korikian Union refers to the nations Engiman, Laychan, and Zabay.)

Hearth

Hearth /hɚθ/ is the red dwarf star that Clova orbits.

The name comes from the English word 'hearth', which refers to the place infront of a fireplace.

The Korikian Union

The Korikian Union /kə.ɹɪk.iɪn/ refers to the grouping of Engiman, Laychan, and Zabay (called this because they are all located in the Korikian Sea). They are the three nations that speak english—albeit in varied dialects.

Despite it relation to where the country's are located, any country located in the Korikian Sea does not immediately become part of the union.

Beau

Beau /boʊ/ is made up mostly of forests, woods, and other vegetation. They value nature and work over most things, and believe that Clova is a goddess.

A lot of people strongly dislike them, because they are what English speakers call Elves. They're usually a little taller than humans. They have long pointed ears, very pale skin, and chiseled angular faces. They speak a language called Beau, which is said to be the most beautiful and smooth sounding language on Clova.

They are one of the only races known to produce sorcerers.

Elve's lifespan is usually about 200 years.

Peace Keepers

(Elven) Peace Keepers /pis ki.pɚ/ are Elves sent from Beau to 'keep the peace' with other nations by doing favors for and helping the royals.

The Silent Wood

The Silent Wood is a forest in Beau, about 60 thousand square feet in size. It is said that it is infested with monsters, which the monsters drove out or killed most of the peaceful creatures, making it eerily silent compared to other forests.

The elves—and other races—have attemped to de-monster the forest, though unfortunately they all failed.

Borok and Karethia

Borok /bɔ.ɹok/ and Karethia's /kə.ɹi.θi.ə/ geography is mostly flat rocky land, with a few patches of trees. They are two of the coldest and least densely populated countries on Clova.

They speak the same language. They are both on the same continent, and in fact were once one country. Though a civil war ended up dividing them into two territories.

The people who live in Borok are called the Boroki /bɔ.ɹo.ki/, and the people in Karethia are called Karethians /kə.ɹi.θi.ən/.

Engiman

The geography of Engiman /en.dʒɪ.mən/ or /eŋ.gɪ.mən/ is mostly lush fields and meadows, with small woods sprinkled about and great rivers flowing through the landscape. Small villages are a common sight near rivers.

It is one of the biggest countries on Clova [about 3 million square-miles.] They speak English natively in an accent similar to the mid-atlantic accent.

They are generally pale—although they redden very easily, whether it be from the sun, alchohol, or a strong emotion. They tend to have brown or blonde hair and brown eyes. There's a common stereotype that they have big noses, but whether or not this is factually correct is to be debated.

Caslogh

Caslogh /kaz.loʊ/ is the city capital of Engiman. The Engiman castle is located here.

Distance, Length and Area

Engiman uses four different distance/length/area measurement units:

League /lig/ (around 3.5 miles)

Double-Pace /də.bʊl peɪs/ (around 5 feet)

Pace /peɪs/ (around 2.5 feet)

Foot /fʊt/ (1 foot)

Finger /fiŋ.ɡɚ/ (around 0.8 inches)

Eqibrism

Eqibrism /e.kɪb.ɹi.ɪz.əm/ is main religion in all English speaking countries on Clova. People who follow the religion are called Eqibrists /ekɪbɹiɪst/.

In Eqibrism, there are two deities: Soll and Nocha. Soll /sɔl/ or /sol/ is the god of day, light, and peace, while Nocha /nəʊtʃə/ is the god of night, dark, and chaos. The religion is focused on balance between the two god's.

It is believed that after death, you are taken away to the High World, which is ruled by the two gods. When the gods are at balance, the High World changes to the perfect place for each individual person. Though if the gods balance were to crumble, the High World would turn into a prison and the mortal world would be split into a million pieces.

Religious leaders are usually healers as well.

Grey

Grey /gɹeɪ/ is town nearby Caslogh. It is often seen as a extension of the capital.

Healer Robes

Healer's robes are made from some of the finest cloth available. Any healer who wears a healer cloak is automatically respected by pretty much everyone around them—everyone from Engiman, anyway—because of the importance they play in religion and day-to-day life.

Houses

Engiman houses are traditionally made from wood planks or logs and stone bricks, supported by a frame of logs. Glass windows are common, though a lot of houses still only have shutters.

Rural Engiman Accent

The accent is often shunned and called 'improper' by the upper-class Engimans.

Some differences between the standard Engiman accent include: /h/ is silent, /t/ is often /ʔ/ or silent, /ʍ/ is /w/, /aɪ/ is /ɑː/, and /eə/ is /ɛː/. 'You' is almost alway pronounced as 'ya', and slang is used a lot more often with speakers of this accent.

Surnames

Surnames are placed after given names. A surname is made up of two parts: the name, and the son/dohtor affix.

The name part comes from the father's name—that is, unless the mother outranks the father. If it's a son, they add the 'son' affix, and if it's a daughter, they add the 'dohtor' affix. For example: Heraldson, Rillendohtor, Juliason, Madriddohtor.

The Grim Guard

The Grim Guard is the unofficial group name given to lower-ranking royal guards of Engiman, due to the fact they are underpaid and often mistreated by high-ups, understandably causinɡ 'ɡrim feelings'.

Vendefiest

Vendefiest /ven.də.fi.əst/ is the period of 30 (Clova) days around the middle of the year in which many Laychanian merchants and traders travel to Caslogh to sell and trade with the townsfolk.

Wilhelm Hill

Wilhelm Hill /wɪl.helm hɪl/ is a village located on a hill of the same name in the far eastern part of Engiman. It is centered around a lake, and is next to a patch of trees.

Words, Slang, Phrases, and Swears

'I'd bet five sterllo that _', or 'I'd bet five sterllo on _'. is a phrase which is usually not literal, and is used as a way to say that you're sure of something.

'When is the time?' is slang for 'What time is it?'

Some words/phrases considered rude, albeit said quite commonly are: imp (as an insult), dreath [/dʒɹeð/ is an offensive name for a poor person, usually used by high-ranking rich people], consarned, son of a gun, ruddy, and—more rarely—sard.

Kalicia

Kalicia /ka.li.ʃi.ə/ is a remote island nation, which the people there speak Kalicien.

The Kaliciens are the race of people who live on the remote island of Kalicia, which is a society of scarce resources and recycling, mostly due to a horrible line of ėn. The main way they get food is hunting, though that is made even harder due to the fact many dangerous creatures lurk in the woods.

Kalicia—which is about 50 hectares of land—is split into three territories. It is quite rocky and hilly, and it is filled to the brim with pine trees.

The Kaliciens have olive skin and (usually long and messy) dark hair and dark eyes. They average at 4'11" in height, which is quite short. This could be due to generations of malnourishment from the difficult environment.

Since the Kaliciens don't have many valuable resources to trade with foreigners (+they are much too busy with civil dispute among the territories) they barely ever come into contact with outsiders. The result of this is that the Kaliciens have little to no outside influence.

Kalicien's value hunting-skill over most things. Usually they use spears and aṅṙubasta to hunt and defend themselves.

Aṅṙubasta

Aṅṙubasta /aŋɾubasta/ (meaning 'metal tooth') are a sort of long (normally 4-5 ft.) Kalicien axe that's commonly made from metal or stone and used for hunting and defense. Some aṅṙubasta are double-sided, although this is less common.

Ėn

Ėn /ən/ are what Kaliciens call their emperors. An ėn historically wears many types of jewelry made from rare and valuable resources, and wields a wooden staff with metal spear-head.

Havėṡtad

Havėṡtad /ha.və.ʃtad/ is the main camp and or village of Kalicia.

Naming System

Given names for a Kalicien are not considered very important, often having simple and straight-forward meanings.

Surnames are heavily intertwined with a Kalicien's job/rank, and if their job changes, so does their surname. For example, if a Kalicien was named Tioltes Irnuþisa, then became the Empress, they would then be named Tioltes Ėnisa.

When a Kalicien reaches the age of around 14-16 (usually depends on whether they are considered mature enough), they are legally an adult and are expected to work. Until then, they are not given a surname, and are usually referred to by only their given name, or in more formal or official circumstances, as 'son/daughter of _'.

Laychan

Laychan /laɪ.ʃeɪn/ or /laɪ.ʃɑn/ mostly has the same geography as Engiman (as it is very close to it), but it is quite smaller and there are a few sandier parts of the country. Royalty lives in the Laychanian /laɪʃeɪniɪn/ castle, which is made from quartz and stone.

Laychan is one of the richest countries on Clova. A large portion of the Laychanians are traders and or merchants.

They speak English. The most notable differences in their dialect from the Engimans are that they pronounce /t̠ʃ/ as /ʃ~tʃ/, /dʒ/ as /ʒ~dʒ/, /ŋ/ as /n/ (only /ŋ/ before /k/ or /g/), /ɛ/ and /ɪ/ may be lowered to /æ/ and /ɛ/, /t/ may be softened to /s/, and 'r' is always rhotic.

Scant

Scant's /skont/ or /skɔnt/ (also spelt 'Skont' [archaic]) land is mostly barren, sandy, and rocky terrain. The country is quite small, centered around the emperor's temple. Houses are built in clusters so that the small communities can help support each other.

They speak Scantese, which is a simplistic and rough sounding language (as described by the inhabitants of Clova).

Scant is known for its ridiculous and oppressing laws and overly severe punishments. The emperor has no care for the 'peasants', and he forces them to give him most of their supplies (food, water, etc.)

Ashundo

Ashundo /æ.ʃun.doʊ/ is the capital city of Scant.

Kjut

Kjut /ki.ut/ is a city on the east coast of Scant.

Laws

  1. Women are forbidden from showing their face in public (they have to wrap it in cloth everywhere besides the eyes), the punishment varies.
  2. All names must be approved by the emperor, and until then, the child is not considered a living thing by law.
  3. Failure to give the emperor produce is seen as theft, and therefore has the same punishment.
  4. If you are found guilty of theft, then you will have your hands tied up in rope and be hung up to dangle for a few days.
  5. Speaking foreign tongues will be punished by cutting off the tongue.
  6. Leaving the country is fiercely prohibited, punishment is often most severe.
  7. Disrespecting the emperor will be punished by death. (Higher-ups often use this as an excuse to execute people that they dislike.)

Zabay

Although Zabay's /zəbaɪ/ geography is pretty similar to Engiman's, it is filled to the brim with towns (also one of the most densely populated countries), and not very many trees grow there.

The leaders of Zabay are cowardice and overall incompetent, although not evil per sé. A lot of Zabayans /zəbajɪn/ openly dislike and disapprove of them.

They speak english as a native tongue. Some notable parts of their dialect is that it's non-rhotic and they pronounce /ð/ as /d/ or /v/, /θ/ as /f/ or /t/, /a/ as /æ~a/, /o/ & /əʊ/ as /ɔ~ɒ/, /ɹ/ as /ɾ/ and /h/ is absent.

A Zabayan usually has tanned skinned and is more muscular than someone from Engiman.

Monuna

The Monuna /mo.nu.nə/ live in little huts surrounding a lake on the very small Monuna island. Not much is known about them.

The Monuna are taller than some of the tallest men, and have leathery jet-black skin. There are many stories that say that some parts of a Monuna can be brewed into potions, but these are generally said to be myth as no-one was ever known to have killed a Monuna.

They seemingly always know when something big is going to happen and what is the best course of action. But—despite how humans usually come to them begging for wisdom and answers—they choose to share this information with certain plants and animals, of which they can communicate.

Their exact lifespan is unknown, but they live for at least a thousand years.

Lifeforms/Types

These are the plentiful and diverse lifeforms—and types of them—of Clova.

Achoiris

An Achoiris /ə.kɔɪ.ɹi/ is a small bird that is sometimes brown, black, or red. They lay round speckled eggs and sound like robins.

spehs

Darwin's Foxes

A Darwin's Fox is a small black-haired canine, weighing around 4.5 pounds. They're commonly owned as pets.

They display no key differences between male and female other than the fact that the male has a broader muzzle.

The males display no territorial behaviour and aren't aggressive towards other males roaming around their territory.

They mostly eat reptiles, beetles, and small invertebrates.

spehs

Fae or Faeries

A Fae /feɪ/ or Faerie /fe.ɹi/ is almost human in appearance, if it wasn't for the colorfully patterned wings on their back. They are also very small—ranging from 6 inches to a foot tall.

They are naturally magical beings, and can do many spells. Although, they are unable to do any exceptionally powerful magic, which means that they aren't very dangerous.

It is common behaviour for them to channel their magic through things, such as twigs and sticks.

spehs

Ǵėliṡ

A Ǵėliṡ /gə.liʃ/ or /ɢə.liʃ/ is an animal that looks like a mix of a deer and an antelope with long hair, but its horns are big and curved like a mountain goat's. They are native to Kalicia.

On average, they are 35 inches tall at the shoulders, weighing 200 pounds. Their thick fur coat usually contains the colors ginger, brown, and white. They are herbivores, and their lifespan is about 6 - 14 Earth years.

They are one of the Kalicien's main sources of meat, and the Kalicien's like to use their horns for decoration, most notably on an ėn's hut.

Goblins

A Goblin /ɡob.lɪn/ [or goblette (feminine)] is a small humanoid [about 3-4 feet tall] with sharp teeth, greenish or bluish skin, and pointed ears.

They are mostly gross and savage thieves.

spehs

Gorgons

A Gorgon /gɔɹ.gən/ is a nasty humanoid that mostly lives in The Silent Wood.

They have snakes for hair, which they can control like limbs, and have the bottom half of a snake.

It is common belief that they have faces so hideously horrifying that it turns humans to stone (most likely originally meaning to paralyze), though because humans interact so little with them, it's hard to say if it's true.

Whenever they hunt for prey, they tend to 'play' with it.

spehs

Harpies

A Harpy /hɑɹ.pi/ has the body of a big black vulture, though the pale and hideous head of a woman. Their razor-sharp talons can cut through leather as easily as butter.

spehs

Harpy Eagles

A Harpy Eagle /hɑɹ.pi i.gʊl/ has slate-black feathers near the top of it, while the bottom half is mostly white. They have a body length of around 40 inches, and a 7 foot wingspan, weighing up to 22 pounds.

They are expert and strong hunters, reported carrying prey weighing up to their own body weight. They usually live deep in the forests.

spehs

Hippocamp

A Hippocamp /hɪ.poʊ.kamp/ (or Seahorse) lives in the sea, and has the upper body of a horse and lower body of a fish.

spehs

Imps

An Imp /ɪmp/ is a mischievous creatures that looks like a small devil and can turn invisible. They are often blamed for bad things that happen, though the damage that they can do is actually quite limited.

Imps are only about a foot tall, with midnight blue skin, bat-like wings, and a devil tail and horns.

'Imp' is also a common insult.

spehs

Lindre

A Lindre /lɪn.dʒɹeɪ/ is a fiery-colored flower that, when consumed, can heal many diseases and illnesses. They are very rare and look like several other common flowers. They are also very expensive.

spehs

Newuks

A Newuk /nju.wək/ (also called an owl) is a type of nocturnal bird.

They are thought to bring bad luck and death, and are often seen as an indication of dark magic.

In Eqibrism, they are believed to have been made by Nocha as way to interact with the mortal world.

spehs

Penumbra Cats

A Penumbra Cat /pə.nəm.bɹə kæt/ (Pen Cat for short) is a big cats with a short bobbed tail and long upper canines like a saber tooth tiger. They usually have black or brown fur and yellow eyes. They are on average around 150 pounds and 50 inches in length.

They appear on almost every continents/island on Clova, discluding the very hot or sandy places, and including Kalicia (called pereð there).

They're very aggressive and territorial, and are often the cause of human death. Their lifespan is about 15 - 20 years.

spehs

Phoenixes

A Phoenix /fin.ɪks/ is a slender and magestic bird. They have yellow, orange, and red feathers, resembling fire.

When a phoenix 'dies', it explodes in show of flames. Then, from the ashes, it is reborn as phoenix chick.

According to Eqibrism, Soll made phoenixes as a way to watch over the mortal world.

spehs

Seers

A Seers /siɹ/ is a special creatures/people with the ability to foretell future events and use simple forms of magic. They are almost all female elves.

spehs

Sidonia

A Sidonia /sɪ.doʊ.njə/ is a common flower with tapered pink petals.

spehs

Stygopads

A Stygopad /ʃtɪ.ɡoʊ.pæd/ is a small and stout toad-like creatures (about 0.8 ft tall) with one eye and one leg/foot.

spehs

Venus Flytraps

A Venus Flytraps /vi.nəs flaɪ.tʃɹæp/ is the only known carnivorous plant. They are very large, ranging from 3 to 8 feet tall.

Once they bite their prey, their digestive acids paralyse them. It only takes a day or two for the prey to be completely digested.

spehs

Warlocks

A Warlock /wɔɹ.lok/ [or witch (feminine)] is a human who can do sorcery. They are rare and almost always related to an elf in some way.

spehs

Woolly Mammoths

A Wooly Mammoth /wʊ.li ma.məθ/ is a big elephant-like creatures covered in brown fur. They have big curved tusks and a long trunk. They live in cold environments.

spehs

Wyvern

A Wyvern /wɪ.vɚn/ is a large serpent-like creatures with two legs and wings. They are cold-blooded and can breath fire.

They are often found hiding away/living/sleeping in caves and other dark holes, rarely protecting stolen treasure. Every now and again, they are disturbed by something or someone and start destroying things in a fit of rage.
